New fieldwork in West Turkana, Kenya, hasidentified evidence of much earlier hominin technological behaviour. Wereport the discovery of Lomekwi 3, a 3.3-million-year-old archaeologicalsite where in situ stone artefacts occur in spatiotemporal associationwith Pliocene hominin fossils in a wooded palaeoenvironment. The Lomekwi3 knappers, with a developing understanding of stone's fractureproperties, combined core reduction with battering activities.
