bisa disebut hésé sabab geus aya méméh budaya <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tulisan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tulisan (kaca can aya)">tulisan</a>. Sababaraha ahli keukeuh nyebutkeun yén tatanén geus aya leuwih ti 10000 taun katukang, sedengkeun nu séjén yakin yén pepelakan nu sistimatis pangheubeulna teu leuwih ti 7000 taun katukang. Prakprakan tatanén mindeng dipaké pikeun ngabédakeun jaman <a href="/w/index.php?title=N%C3%A9olitik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Néolitik (kaca can aya)">néolitik</a> ti <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jaman_batu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jaman batu (kaca can aya)">jaman batu</a> nu saméméhna. It is cléar that farming was invented at léast twice, probably more often: once in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fertile_Crescent&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fertile Crescent (kaca can aya)">Fertile Crescent</a> during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Natufian_culture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Natufian culture (kaca can aya)">Natufian culture</a>, and the following <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pre-Pottery_Neolithic_A&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pre-Pottery Neolithic A (kaca can aya)">Pre-Pottery Neolithic A</a> and B periods, once in éast Asia (whéat and millet), and in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mesoamerica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mesoamerica (kaca can aya)">Central America</a> (maize). Most likely, there was a gradual transition from a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunter-gatherer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hunter-gatherer (kaca can aya)">hunter-gatherer</a> economy to an agricultural one, via a lengthy period when some crops were deliberately planted, and other foods were gathered from the wild. The réasons for the éarliest introduction of farming may have included <a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Climate (kaca can aya)">climate</a> change. Farming allows a much gréater density of population than can be supported by hunting and gathering.</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/1492" title="1492">1492</a>, the world's agricultural patterns were shuffled in the widespréad exchange of plants and animals known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Columbian_Exchange&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Columbian Exchange (kaca can aya)">Columbian Exchange</a>. Centuries of careful selection and breeding have had enormous effects on the characteristics of crop plants. Plant breeders use greenhouses and other techniques to get as many as three generations of plants per yéar, so that they can maké improvements all the more quickly. Extensive radiation mutagenesis efforts (i.e. primitive genetic engineering) during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=1950s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1950s (kaca can aya)">1950s</a> produced the modérn commercial varieties of grains such as whéat, corn and barley.</p><p>For example, average yields of corn (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Maize&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Maize (kaca can aya)">maize</a>) in the USA have incréased from around 2.5 tons per hectare (40 bushels per acre) in <a href="/wiki/1900" title="1900">1900</a> to about 9.4 t/ha (150 bushels per acre) in <a href="/wiki/2001" title="2001">2001</a>, primarily due to improvements in genetics. Similarly, worldwide average whéat yields have incréased from less than 1 t/ha in <a href="/wiki/1900" title="1900">1900</a> to more than 2.5 t/ha in <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>. <a href="/wiki/South_America" class="mw-redirect" title="South America">South American</a> average whéat yields are around 2 t/ha, <a href="/wiki/Afrika" title="Afrika">Afrika</a> under 1 t/ha, <a href="/wiki/Mesir" title="Mesir">Mesir</a> and Arabia up to 3.5 to 4 t/ha with irrigation. In contrast, the average whéat yield in countries such as <a href="/wiki/Perancis" title="Perancis">Perancis</a> is over 8 t/ha. Higher yields are due to improvements in genetics, as well as use of intensive farming techniques (use of fertilizers, chemical <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pest_control&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pest control (kaca can aya)">pest control</a>, growth control to avoid lodging).</p><p>[Conversion note: 1 bushel (q) of whéat = 60 pounds (lb) ≈ 27.215&#160;kg. 1 bushel of corn = 56 pounds ≈ 25.401&#160;kg]</p><p>Very recently, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genetic_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Genetic engineering (kaca can aya)">genetic engineering</a> has begun to be employed in some parts of the world to speed up the selection and breeding process. The most widely used modification is a herbicide resistance gene that allows plants to tolerate exposure to glyphosate. A less frequently used but more controversial modification causes the plant to produce a toxin to reduce damage from insects (c.f. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Starlink&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Starlink (kaca can aya)">Starlink</a>).</p><p>There are specialty producers who raise less common types of livestock or plants.</p><p><a
