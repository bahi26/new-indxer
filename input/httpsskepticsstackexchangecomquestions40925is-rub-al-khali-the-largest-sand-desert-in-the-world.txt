is still behind the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thar_Desert" rel="noreferrer">Thar Desert</a>, as “Most of the desert is covered by huge shifting sand dunes that receive sediments from the alluvial plains and the coast.”</li></ul></li></ol><hr><p>All numbers come from <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_deserts_by_area" rel="noreferrer">Wikipedia’s list of largest deserts</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rub%27_al_Khali" rel="noreferrer">Wikipedia’s article on Rub' al Khali</a>, and <a href="https://skeptics.stackexchange.com/a/40928/6207">DavePhD’s answer, for Erg Chech</a>, though I have created my own lists to emphasize various points.</p><p>Note that these rankings are fairly arbitrary: I just used Wikipedia’s list, added in Rub' al Khali, Great Sandy–Gibson as a single desert, and Erg Chech, to address the desert subsets that have been discussed in answers here, and applied my own (fairly arbitrary) filtering to the first list to produce the second and third. There is nothing special about my rankings; you could easily come up with different rules for what does or doesn’t count, and achieve different rankings. The point is that you have to pay very close attention to exactly what competition someone is coming in 1st in—qualifiers on it may make the achievement less than it first appears.</p><p>Also note that the numbers don’t always agree: <a href="https://skeptics.stackexchange.com/a/40928/6207">DavePhD’s answer</a> has a larger area for the Great Sandy–Gibson than Wikipedia does (possibly by including areas between the two deserts that would not be considered part of either when separated), as well as a smaller area for Rub' al Khali. Natural borders are not always fixed, and are not always clear, hard lines—differences of opinion in where the limit is, what does or doesn’t count as still being part of the same desert, etc. all can come into play, to say nothing of the possibility that they can and do shift over time.</p>    </div>    <div class="grid mb0 fw-wrap ai-start jc-end gs8 gsy">    <div class="grid--cell mr16" And you do not call an entire desert a sand desert just because a part of it — in this case only 1/5 of the total area — is a sand desert. </p><p><a href="https://i.stack.imgur.com/Bq5OB.jpg" rel="noreferrer"><img src="https://i.stack.imgur.com/Bq5OB.jpg" alt="enter image description here"></a></p><p><em>This is a desert. The image is taken at the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dome_C" rel="noreferrer">Dome C Station</a> in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica" rel="noreferrer">Antartica</a> <sub>(<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polar_desert#/media/File:AntarcticaDomeCSnow.jpg" rel="noreferrer">image source</a>)</sub></em></p><p>The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Desert" rel="noreferrer">Arabian Desert</a> is not just a sand desert, it consists of several types of desert. To say that The Arabian Desert is "the largest sand desert" implies that the Arabian Desert is a sand desert in its entirety, which is not true. A part of it is the world's largest sand desert, but not all of it.</p><p>By comparison: you would never say that the Arabian Desert is the world's largest oasis, just because <strong>one part of it</strong> — <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Hasa" rel="noreferrer">Al-Hasa (الأحساء)</a> — <strong>is</strong> the world's largest oasis. </p>    </div>    <div class="grid mb0 fw-wrap
